# Sydney, Layered

An interactive map of historical Sydney: jump through time across historical aerial
imagery of the city, with stories, buildings and people pinned to places. Coverage:
**greater Sydney** — Kamay/Botany Bay to Parramatta, Manly to Rookwood, 1770 to today.

## Run it

No build step. Serve the `web/` folder and open it:

```sh
python3 -m http.server 8000 -d web
# → http://localhost:8000
```

## What's here

```
web/                    Frontend — vanilla JS + MapLibre GL (CDN), no build step
  index.html
  app.js                Time-machine year layers, era filters, story pins/tooltips
  data/stories.geojson  The story content — edit this to add/curate stories
pipeline/               Python tooling for sourcing layers and story material
  fetch_trove.py        Pull candidate newspaper articles from the Trove API
  find_allmaps.py       Find georeferenced historical maps of Sydney on Allmaps
  fetch_coverage.py     Probe each year's tile cache to map its real coverage
```

## Time travel

The primary control is seven **era chips** — Indigenous, Colonial, Victorian,
Federation, Wartime, Modern, Now. Picking an era shows its best rendition of Sydney
*and* filters the map and sidebar to only the people, places and stories of that
period (each feature carries a `periods` array; features can belong to several).

Each era is colour-coded (pins, sidebar, legend and chips all match) and pairs an
aerial rendition with a treatment — no map overlays, after testing showed they
read as confusing:

Each pre-photography rendition shows Sydney in its era's **final year**:

| Era | Colour | Rendition |
|---|---|---|
| Indigenous (to 1787) | ochre | **Synthesised "Country" satellite** — real coastline/water, all land re-wilded |
| Colonial 1788–1840 | terracotta | **Synthesised reconstruction, 1840** — settlement footprints + roads over Country |
| Victorian 1841–1899 | green | **Synthesised reconstruction, 1899** — footprints, roads + railways |
| Federation 1900–1918 | gold | **Synthesised reconstruction, 1919** — the tram/rail suburbs of the Commonwealth |
| Wartime 1919–1945 | khaki | **1943 aerials, colourised** (`pipeline/era_colorize.py`): real wartime luminance + colour transferred from today's imagery of the same streets, regional-tint fallback where the city changed, vector-water chroma, 1919-reconstruction infill beyond the survey edge. Raw B&W remains as the `1943` layer (tours use it) |
| Modern 1946–2005 | steel blue | 1975 aerials, gap-filled by 1970 (north-west corridor) and 1965 (CBD) |
| Now | — | Today's mosaic, every pin shown |

The pre-photography renditions are generated by `pipeline/era_tiles.py` on the
principle that **coastlines don't change — only land cover does**: a land/water
mask is classified from today's imagery (boats/bridges erased by mask dilation +
water blurring), all land is re-wilded with bush texture quilted from the
national parks inside the frame (today's bush *is* the pre-1788 land cover), and
the dated settlement footprints/roads/rail from `evolution.geojson` are
composited over it — farmland texture from today's rural fringe, town grain
sampled from real 1943 imagery, sepia-toned. Output is an **XYZ tile pyramid
(z10–z15 native, ~4.8 m/px)** under `web/tiles/era-{country,1840,1899}/` —
~72 MB per era. Texture decisions are deterministic functions of global pixel
position, so tiles join seamlessly. Schematic by design (the captions say
"reconstruction, not a photograph"); improve by refining the footprint polygons
and re-running (~20 min). `pipeline/era_synth.py` is the older single-image
prototype. The evolution animation uses the same renditions pre-1943.

Two early maps remain georeferenced and on disk (`web/data/overlays/`,
`pipeline/georef_1802.py` / `georef_1854.py` — Lesueur 1802 at ≈27 m RMS,
Woolcott & Clarke 1854 at ≈90 m RMS) but are not currently shown; re-add them to
`YEARS` in `app.js` to bring them back (e.g. as an optional "see the survey" toggle).

Under the hood the imagery years (1854, 1943, 1955, 1965, 1970, 1994, 2005, today)
still exist as layers — tours can pin a specific year. All year layers stay mounted
at opacity 0 so tiles preload and era jumps are instant. Each survey's real
footprint is measured by `pipeline/fetch_coverage.py`
(it probes the tile cache and writes `web/data/coverage.json`); the app dims the map
outside the active rendition's coverage and draws its boundary as a dashed line.
Re-run the script whenever a tile year is added to `YEARS` in `web/app.js`
(note: it rewrites coverage.json — re-add the 1854 entry via the bounds file).
Tile layers are free public NSW Government services — verified to
have real coverage over both the CBD and Bondi:

| Year | Service |
|---|---|
| 1943 | `sixmaps/sydney1943` on maps.six.nsw.gov.au (z ≤ 17) |
| 1955, 1965, 1970, 1975, 1994, 2005 | `Hosted/HistoricalImagery<year>` on portal.spatial.nsw.gov.au (z ≤ 18) |

Metro coverage at z14 (probed): 1975 = 771/1036 tiles (best mid-era), 2005 = 872,
2004 = 799, 1998 = 740, 1955 = 430, 1965 = 411 (CBD-centred — complements 1975),
1943 = 374, 1994 = 339, 1996 = 325, 1971 = 334, 1970 = 223. A `fallback` field on a
`YEARS` entry layers a second survey beneath it to fill gaps (used for 1975+1965).
| Today | `public/NSW_Imagery` on maps.six.nsw.gov.au (z ≤ 19) |

Also available with Sydney coverage if more steps are wanted: 1971, 1996, 2004.
Years that probe as *empty* over Sydney (blank/404 tiles): 1947, 1951, 1954, 1966,
1969, 1972, 1975 (Bondi only), 1976, 1978–1982 (partial), 1989 (Bondi only), 1998 (Bondi only).

Street/place labels are a CARTO `dark_only_labels` raster overlay, toggleable.

## Evolution — "Watch Sydney grow"

The ▶ button under the time-travel chips plays a 30-second sweep from 1770 to
today (inspired by Budget Direct's 250-years animation): a large year counter
ticks over while

- the satellite base steps through ten visual stages (bush-green Country →
  era-faded 1943 → 1955 → 1965 → 1970 → 1994 → 2005 → today),
- a hand-authored **reconstruction layer** (`web/data/evolution.geojson`) draws
  the growing urban footprint in translucent amber plus the great roads and
  railways as their years arrive (Parramatta Rd 1794, the 1855 railway, the
  Illawarra/North Shore lines, the Bridge in 1932…), and
- all 136 pins accumulate chronologically (`appear` year: stories by event year,
  buildings by construction, people at adulthood).

The footprint polygons are schematic — authored from era knowledge at metro zoom,
not parcel data. Refine them in `evolution.geojson` (each feature: `kind` =
footprint/road/rail, `from` = first year shown).

## Tours

`web/data/tours.json` defines guided tour tracks. Each tour is a sequence of stops
referencing existing features by `"type:id"` (`story:`, `building:`, `person:`) with
an optional `note` — the connective narration shown above the stop's full card.
Starting a tour draws a dotted route with numbered stop badges, switches the time
machine to the tour's `year` (restored on exit), and opens stop 1 with
Back / Next / End controls; the close × also ends the tour. Tours with broken refs
are skipped with a console warning, so content edits can't crash the tour list.

Pin positions were visually audited by rendering every feature on its satellite
tile (`/tmp/audit_pins.py` pattern) — re-run after bulk coordinate edits.

Current tracks: Colonial Sydney, Tour The Rocks, Dark Sydney (true crime & ghosts),
Rebels & Bohemians (ends at Mardi Gras), Sporting Sydney.

## Suburbs

The sidebar's suburb search covers all 803 gazetted suburbs of greater Sydney
(`web/data/suburbs.json`, built by `pipeline/build_suburbs.py` from the NSW
Administrative Boundaries suburb layer). Selecting one:

- fetches its official boundary live from the NSW FeatureServer (CORS-enabled)
  and draws it on the map,
- zooms to its bbox,
- shows a suburb card with its naming/formation history — **all 803 suburbs
  have one**: ~570 authored from general knowledge, ~230 researched by agents
  against the NSW Geographical Names Board register (uncertain etymologies are
  honestly flagged as such rather than invented). Histories live in
  `pipeline/suburb_histories.json`. The knowledge-authored ones have not had a
  dedicated fact-check pass yet — name etymologies are folk-tale-prone; treat
  the GNB register as the authority — and a count of its features, and
- spatially filters the map and sidebar to features inside the boundary
  (true point-in-polygon, combined with the era/theme filters).

The × in the search row or on the card exits suburb mode. To add a history,
add an entry to `HISTORIES` in `build_suburbs.py` (uppercase gazetted name)
and re-run it.

## Filters

The sidebar's Filters panel combines three dimensions:

- **Show** — toggle the Stories / Buildings / People layers on and off independently.
- **Theme** — every feature carries one of ten themes: First Nations,
  Colony & Convicts, Politics & Protest, War & Defence, Industry & Transport,
  Sport & Leisure, Arts & Ideas, Science & Medicine, Migration, Crime & Catastrophe.

Theme combines with the active time-travel era (e.g. *Victorian + Crime &
Catastrophe*), and both apply to the map and the sidebar lists. Pins scale with
zoom so the metro-wide view stays readable. Pin colours still encode the four
historical content eras (see legend).

## Eras

Stories are grouped and colour-coded into four eras (filter chips in the sidebar):

| Era | Range | Pin colour |
|---|---|---|
| Colonial Sydney | 1788–1840 | terracotta |
| Victorian Sydney | 1840–1900 | green |
| Federation Sydney | 1900–1920 | gold |
| Modern Sydney | 1920–now | steel blue |

Aerial photography only exists from 1943, so the Colonial/Victorian/Federation eras
need **georeferenced map and artist-rendition layers**. That's what
`pipeline/find_allmaps.py` is for: it searches the Allmaps API for city-scale
georeferenced maps intersecting Sydney and emits ready-to-use `allmaps.xyz` XYZ tile
URLs. Targets worth hunting for: the 1888 *City and Suburbs of Sydney* map, the
1855–65 trigonometrical survey sheets, and 19th-century subdivision plans
(State Library of NSW). The City of Sydney Archives' [Historical Atlas](https://archives.cityofsydney.nsw.gov.au/pages/historicalatlas)
is another source for CBD-area georeferenced maps.

## Buildings

`web/data/buildings.geojson` holds significant historical buildings, drawn as hollow
era-coloured rings (stories are solid dots). Clicking one opens a richer card:
hero image, **timeline** of the building's lives (`timeline: [{year, event}]`),
original vs current use, a few stories, and a Trove link. The era filter applies
to buildings too. Like the stories, the 12 seed buildings are drafted from general
knowledge — verify before publishing.

## People

`web/data/people.geojson` holds the human layer: individuals anchored to the place
that defined them, drawn as dark dots with era-coloured rings. Each has `lived`,
`role`, `place`, a multi-paragraph `body`, a portrait and a Trove link — from
Bennelong and Barangaroo on their headlands through Mary Reibey, Caroline Chisholm
and Louisa Lawson to the razor queens, Mr Eternity, Jack Mundey and Juanita Nielsen.
Barangaroo deliberately has no image: no portrait of her is known to exist.
Same draft-content caveat as everything else.

## Story content workflow

Stories live in `web/data/stories.geojson`, one Feature per story with
`title`, `year`, `era`, `summary`, `body`, `source` properties and a Point geometry.
`era` must be one of the four era names above. Hovering a pin shows the summary;
clicking shows the full story.

Optional media properties bring a story alive:

- `image`, `image_caption`, `image_link` — a period photograph/painting shown at
  the top of the popup. Current images are public-domain works hotlinked from
  Wikimedia Commons (`image_link` points to the Commons page for attribution).
- `papers` — a Trove digitised-newspapers search URL (keyword + decade), rendered
  as a "From the papers" link. With a Trove API key this can be upgraded to
  embedded clipping images via `pipeline/fetch_trove.py`.

> **Content status (June 2026):** all 177 features were drafted from general
> knowledge (plus Harbour Trust source pages), then fact-checked in two passes by
> parallel research agents verifying load-bearing claims (dates, numbers, names,
> superlatives) against web sources — 62 corrections applied in total. Narrative
> colour and minor details remain unverified; treat listed sources as the
> authority for anything consequential. Every pin has been visually audited
> against satellite imagery at z16.

To find new material: `pipeline/fetch_trove.py` searches Trove's digitised
newspapers (free API key required) and dumps candidate articles as JSON for you
to curate into stories.
